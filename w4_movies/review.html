Review of Battleship

Bradley Coppinger review on August 10, 2023

★★★★★

If you are looking for an action-pact movie, look no further. This is by far one of the best "alien invasion" movies to date. It brings in a sense of realism, and how I think the Navy would actually react to an alien response such as this. The charatcrs provide a great sense of humor, and play their roles very well. The CGI is also worth note; as it is very complex and life like for a movie filmed in 2012.


As far as negatives go, I only have a few. Sometimes the actors break characters and its easy to tell you are watching a film. However, many of them make up for this and you are right back in the action. This film also adds a love story that is followe dthroughout; which does appeal to a wider audience. This does fit the film and is nicley done.

Overall, for the genre of the movie and style, I award this film a 5/5. I only wish they did not cancel the Battleship 2. I feel like with streaming services being so accessible, it is easy for eveyrone to watch and fall in live with this film. I only hope in the future Hollywood can take advantage from this wonderful production and put it forward to other projects.



Triva and facts about Empire Records

  • The veterans of the U.S.S. Missouri that Stone Hopper mentions are real life vets of the U.S.S. Missouri. Some served in World War II.
  • USS Missouri was taken out of port for one day in 2010 for filming purposes crewed by US Navy personnel.
  • The scene in the rehabilitation clinic was actually filmed at the Center for the Intrepid in San Antonio, Texas, using actual wounded warriors for extras.
  • The inspiration for the design of the alien ships was waterbugs.
  • RIMPAC is short for Rim of the Pacific Exercise.
